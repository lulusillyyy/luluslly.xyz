# Keyboards!!!
As maybe like... one of you guys may be able to surmise, I like keyboards. (*I'll probably post an article going over my keyboard/mouse collection, although I wouldn't tell you guys to be optimistic, as I don't have too many interesting entries.*) 

In recent keyboard news, I have done some work on my 36-key [Corne](https://luluslly.xyz/post/2025/08/02/on-getting-my-first-split-mech-keyboard/) (pictured below) to make it a little easier for me to use normally, and it has made me realise that I generally prefer lower profile keys over standard MX switches. Despite this, I made the decision to buy a used [Kinesis Advantage 2](https://kinesis-ergo.com/shop/advantage2) off of eBay. While I am stoked, I didn't realise until after I made my purchase that Kinesis' ergonomic keyboards -- *besides the [Advantage 360 Pro](https://kinesis-ergo.com/shop/adv360pro)* -- does not support QMK/ZMK firmware without replacing the internal microcontroller; meaning I cannot have my custom keybindings with home row mods, layers, and combos without having to use software remapping tools like [Kanata](https://github.com/jtroo/kanata). I suppose I will have to work on creating an efficient layout once I get it in the mail. I'll definitely be giving the board a written review.

# On Chainsaw Man Ending

As some may know, the popular manga *Chainsaw Man* ended on March 24th with its final chapter. If you couldn't tell by my home page, I like CSM and am fairly invested in it. So when I read the final chapter last night, I was left with mixed feelings.

<details>
    <summary>WARNING! I'm gonna spoil the FUCK out of the manga so BEWARE!!!</summary>
    <p>
        Seeing Denji basically have his life mostly reset back to pre-gun devil arc (with continuity changes) was kind of interesting, but it felt like it all came out of nowhere. It's obvious that if we saw Denji return to the original timeline ALIVE he would simply end up dying somehow since he no longer has Pochita; not to mention the world being in absolute chaos at that point. I feel even *that* would've brought enough closure in the sense that things would be more coherent. At the same time though, I feel as though this ending isn't entirely devoid of meaning either. Pochita said it succinctly before eating himself: Denji can only find happiness in calamity. His yearning for comfort through putting himself in such situations essentially brought forth the end of the world, and in the end he needs to find meaning in living a simple life. This is what makes Pochita's sacrifice noble and worthwhile. While Denji will likely never be Chainsaw Man again, he will at least be able to build a life that he is comfortable with. It's a nice send off, but gives the ending more of a fanservicey feel to it, rather than a grounded conclusion with more significant loose ends tied off for good measure. I guess the mythos within CSM prohibit things being completely answerable though. It sort of defeats the purpose of leaving things up to interpretation, no? 
        Anyhow, 10/10 manga, would suffer again.
